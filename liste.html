Here's the history.

List√© was created as a project between me and two other teammates for the Swift Innovators' Summit, the end-year summit held to conclude the yearly Swift Accelerator Programme.

Ideation began in mid to late 2019. While we were aware of the different to-do lists that existed out there, we realised that many of them were overly complicated with things we often didn't need. Our idea was to create a to-do list app that focused on simplicity; all you had to fill up is a task's name, an optional description, and a due date.

Over the next month or two, I helmed the main responsibility as the primary app developer for our team. Working individually while receiving feedback and updates from my teammates allowed a smooth development process for the app. The app was final and ready to be published by October of the year.

In 2020, I began the process of re-writing the app from the ground up. List√© version 2.0 has since been completed and is now live on the Play Store. While our team hasn't worked together on this re-write as we have before, I did my best to keep the original intention and design of the app intact.

Here come the technicalities.

This project utilises a some cool technologies, and I utilised them the best I could to what I knew to make the end result the best effort. This app uses the following tech stack listed below.


Swift

I created this app using the Apple-created programming language of Swift and the Xcode IDE. I created the app using the Storyboards variant of an Xcode project, using a mix of visual UI drag-and-drop work with a lot of manual programming. In addition, I used the Cocoapods dependency manager to manage external libraries required.


Firebase

For authentication and data storage, I used the Firebase platform to handle these areas relatively easily. Its simple-to-understand documentation alongside tutorial videos accelerated my learning of how to use it. Initially starting out with Realtime Database, my team made the decision to use Cloud Firestore instead. We also used Authentication for, well, authentication.
